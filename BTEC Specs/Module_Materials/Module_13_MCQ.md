# Module 13: Safety Standards & Design for Safety
## Multiple Choice Questions

**Instructions:** Choose the best answer for each question.

---

**1. What is ISO 10218?**
   a) A robot speed standard
   b) Industrial robot safety standard
   c) A robot color standard
   d) A robot cost standard

**2. What is a safe stop?**
   a) A controlled stop that maintains safety
   b) An emergency stop
   c) The robot stopping permanently
   d) The robot moving slowly

**3. What is an emergency stop?**
   a) Immediate stop in dangerous situations
   b) A slow stop
   c) The robot continuing
   d) The robot moving faster

**4. Why are joint limits a safety feature?**
   a) To prevent damage and unsafe positions
   b) To make it faster
   c) To make it slower
   d) They're not a safety feature

**5. Why are speed caps important?**
   a) To prevent dangerous high-speed movements
   b) To make it faster
   c) To make it slower
   d) They're not important

**6. What should happen on loss of communication?**
   a) Robot should stop safely
   b) Robot should continue
   c) Robot should move faster
   d) Nothing happens

**7. What should happen on power failure?**
   a) Robot should stop safely (fail-safe)
   b) Robot should continue
   c) Robot should move faster
   d) Nothing happens

**8. What are conservative defaults?**
   a) Safe default settings that prevent accidents
   b) Fast default settings
   c) Random settings
   d) No defaults

**9. Why is torque on/off control important for safety?**
   a) To prevent accidental movement
   b) To make it faster
   c) To make it slower
   d) It's not important

**10. What is fail-safe design?**
   a) System fails to a safe state
   b) System fails dangerously
   c) System never fails
   d) System fails randomly

---

## Answer Key

1. **b)** Industrial robot safety standard
2. **a)** A controlled stop that maintains safety
3. **a)** Immediate stop in dangerous situations
4. **a)** To prevent damage and unsafe positions
5. **a)** To prevent dangerous high-speed movements
6. **a)** Robot should stop safely
7. **a)** Robot should stop safely (fail-safe)
8. **a)** Safe default settings that prevent accidents
9. **a)** To prevent accidental movement
10. **a)** System fails to a safe state
